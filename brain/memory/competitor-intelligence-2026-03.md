### Snapshot Date: March 2, 2026

## Executive Summary

The personalized medicine and telehealth space is consolidating fast. The GLP-1 compounding window is closing (FDA resolved the semaglutide shortage Feb 21, 2025), forcing every player to pivot or die. Hims & Hers is the runaway revenue leader at $2.35B in 2025. Ro is well-funded but stagnant at a $7B valuation from 2022 with no new raise. The TRT/men's health vertical is fragmenting into a dozen sub-$200/month commodity clinics. The AI-native healthcare platform space is flush with capital but selling to health systems, not consumers.

**Our clearest opportunity:** The intersection of personalized medicine + compounding pharmacy + AI-orchestrated care + brick-and-mortar integration — a lane none of the pure-play telehealth companies occupy.

## VERTICAL 1: Telehealth Platforms

### 🔴 Ro (formerly Roman) — PRIORITY TARGET

**Overview:** Founded 2017, New York. Rebranded from Roman to Ro to signal broader ambition beyond men's health. Raised **$1.03B total**, last valued at **$7B (2022)** — no new raise since, suggesting either profitability or stagnation.

**Service Catalog:**
- **Ro Body** — Weight loss program: GLP-1s (Wegovy, Ozempic, Zepbound, Saxenda) + health coaching
- **Sexual Health** — ED (sildenafil from $4/dose, tadalafil, Cialis, Viagra), premature ejaculation (Roman Swipes $2.75/swipe), genital herpes (valacyclovir)
- **Hair Loss** — Finasteride + minoxidil topical
- **Fertility** — Modern Fertility hormone tests (acquired)
- **Skincare** — Custom Rx skincare (Ro Derm)
- **Testosterone Support** — OTC supplement only (ashwagandha, zinc, magnesium, D3, maca) — NOT prescription TRT
- **General Health** — OTC vitamins, at-home lab tests

**Pricing:**
- Online consultations: **free** in most cases
- Ro Body Program: **$45/month (first month), $145/month thereafter** — medication not included
- Sildenafil: from **$4/dose**
- Roman Swipes: **$27/month**
- Testosterone Support supplement: **$29–$35/month**
- No insurance accepted (except GLP-1 insurance concierge navigation)
- HSA/FSA eligible

**GLP-1 / Compounding Situation:**
- Ro was heavily invested in compounded semaglutide during the shortage window
- FDA resolved semaglutide shortage **Feb 21, 2025** — compounders legally required to stop
- Ro pivoted: **Jan 5, 2026** — launched Wegovy® Pill integration with Novo Nordisk at "lowest available cash pay price"
- Court upheld FDA's removal of semaglutide from shortage list (Jan 2026)
- This is a **significant business disruption** — compounded GLP-1 was a major revenue driver

**Tech Platform:**
- Web + mobile app (4.8 stars on Apple App Store, 2.9k+ reviews)
- Async messaging with care team (24/7)
- Network of nationwide pharmacies (not in-house)
- No proprietary compounding pharmacy

**Weaknesses:**
- No insurance coverage (pure cash-pay)
- Mixed online reputation — no refunds or returns policy
- No prescription TRT (only OTC supplement) — major gap vs. competitors
- Compounded GLP-1 revenue disrupted by FDA shortage resolution
- Valuation ($7B) likely stale — no new capital in 3+ years
- No brick-and-mortar presence
- Coaching model criticized as insufficient for lasting behavior change
- No peptides, thyroid, or advanced hormone optimization

**Positioning:** "One-stop telehealth shop" — broad but shallow. Consumer-friendly UX, strong brand recognition, but increasingly commoditized.

## VERTICAL 2: Compounding Pharmacy / Rx

### 🔴 The GLP-1 Compounding Collapse (Industry-Wide Threat/Opportunity)

**Key event:** FDA resolved semaglutide shortage Feb 21, 2025. Tirzepatide removed Oct 2024.

**Impact on compounders:**
- **Hallandale Pharmacy** — discontinued semaglutide, now limited GLP-1 offerings
- **Empower Pharmacy** — confirmed stopping compounded semaglutide (as of Feb 2026)
- Court upheld FDA decision Jan 2026 — no legal reprieve for compounders

**What this means for us:** The compounded GLP-1 gold rush is over for pure-play pharmacies. Players with **brand-name GLP-1 access + integrated telehealth** (like Ro's Wegovy pill deal with Novo Nordisk) have an edge. Our pharmacy relationships (DiRX, Thrive Rx, OK Capsule, Spatil) need to be evaluated for what they can still offer legally.

### Thrive Rx
- Known partner/competitor in our ecosystem
- Compounding pharmacy with telehealth relationships
- Status post-GLP-1 ruling: needs monitoring

### DiRX (jon@getdrx.com)
- Active integration in our system
- Discount medication platform — not a compounder, more of a PBM/discount card play
- Complementary rather than competitive

### Empower Pharmacy
- 503A compounding pharmacy + FDA-registered 503B facility
- Major player, now pulling back from GLP-1 compounding
- Still active in TRT, peptides, other compounds

### Hallandale Pharmacy
- Discontinued semaglutide (Feb 2026)
- Still active in other compounding categories

## VERTICAL 3: Men's Health / TRT / Peptides

### 🔴 Defy Medical — PRIORITY TARGET

**Overview:** Industry-leading HRT/TRT clinic. Telemedicine + in-person (Tampa, FL). Led by Dr. Justin Saya. One of the most-cited names in "best online TRT clinic" roundups.

**Service Catalog:**
- TRT (testosterone replacement therapy)
- BHRT (bioidentical hormone replacement)
- Peptide therapy
- Thyroid support
- TriMix (ED injection) — consultation now $79 (discounted)
- Integrative therapies
- Comprehensive blood testing + lab analysis
- Long-format doctor consultations

**Pricing:**
- Estimated **$200–$300/month** depending on treatment plan
- ~**$3,000/year** (per TRT Nation competitive breakdown)
- No flat-rate pricing — billed by service
- Labs and follow-ups billed separately
- Higher upfront costs + longer onboarding than competitors

**Strengths:**
- Deepest treatment menu in the TRT space
- Physician-led with detailed consultations
- Peptides + thyroid + advanced HRT — not just testosterone
- Long-term optimization focus
- Strong brand reputation in the HRT/TRT community
- In-person option (Tampa) differentiates from pure-play telehealth

**Weaknesses:**
- No flat-rate pricing — confusing cost structure
- Higher cost than commodity TRT clinics
- Longer onboarding — not ideal for quick-start patients
- Communication and shipping delay complaints (Trustpilot)
- No AI-native platform or tech differentiation
- No weight loss / GLP-1 program
- Geographic concentration (in-person only Tampa)

**Competitive Position vs. Us:** Defy is the "premium concierge" TRT clinic. We can beat them on: integrated platform (Impetus One), AI-driven care orchestration, broader service catalog (adding GLP-1/weight loss), and potentially price transparency.

## Threat Assessment: Who's Making Aggressive Moves Right Now

### 🔥 HIGH THREAT

**Hims & Hers** — $2.35B revenue, 59% growth, 2.5M subscribers, Super Bowl advertising. The 800-lb gorilla. Moving into everything. Their scale creates pricing power we can't match head-on. **Strategy:** Don't compete on breadth — compete on depth and personalization.

**Henry Meds** — Cheapest all-inclusive TRT ($129/mo with labs). Aggressive pricing is commoditizing the entry-level TRT market. **Strategy:** We need to be clearly premium and differentiated, not price-competitive with them.

**Ro + Novo Nordisk partnership** — Ro's Jan 2026 Wegovy pill deal gives them brand-name GLP-1 access at "lowest cash-pay price." This is a smart pivot post-compounding shutdown. **Strategy:** Watch this closely — if they can undercut on branded GLP-1 pricing, that's a threat to weight loss programs.

### 🟡 MEDIUM THREAT

**Defy Medical** — Premium TRT/HRT, but no tech platform, no weight loss, no AI. Beatable on platform and service breadth. **Strategy:** Directly competitive — target their patients who want more than just TRT.

**Maximus** — Fertility-preserving TRT niche is smart. $99–$300/mo range. Growing community ("Tribe" branding). **Strategy:** We can offer the same protocols plus more.

**Commure** ($200M raised) — AI scribing → platform play. If they become the infrastructure layer for telehealth, they could power our competitors. **Strategy:** Monitor; consider whether Impetus One can partner or differentiate clearly.

### 🟢 LOWER THREAT (for now)

**Teladoc** — Enterprise/B2B, not DTC. Declining revenue in consumer segment.

**LifeMD** — Smaller, slower growth. Not an aggressive mover.

**Pure-play TRT commodity clinics** (TRT Nation, Fountain, Rex MD) — Competing on price alone. Not a strategic threat if we stay premium.

## Positioning Gaps — Where We Win

### 1. 🏆 The "Full Stack" Personalized Medicine Platform
No competitor combines: AI-powered intake → telehealth consultation → prescription management → compounding pharmacy integration → in-person option → ongoing optimization. Impetus One + our pharmacy relationships is the only platform that can do this end-to-end.

### 2. 🏆 Prescription TRT + Peptides + Weight Loss in One Place
Ro has no prescription TRT. Hims has no prescription TRT. Defy has no weight loss. Henry Meds has no peptides. **We can be the only provider that does all three** — and that's a massive differentiator for the optimization-focused patient.

### 3. 🏆 AI-Native Care Orchestration
Impetus One's PracticeSYNC is the only AI-powered clinical platform in this space that's ours. Every competitor is running on generic EHRs or basic apps. This is a tech moat if we execute.

### 4. 🏆 Brick-and-Mortar + Telehealth Hybrid
Defy Medical has Tampa. Everyone else is pure telehealth. Patients who want in-person labs, injections, or consultations have almost nowhere to go. A hybrid model is a genuine differentiator.

### 5. 🏆 Post-Compounding GLP-1 Positioning
With Empower and Hallandale out of compounded semaglutide, the compounding pharmacy landscape is reshuffling. Players with brand-name GLP-1 access (Ro/Novo Nordisk) or tirzepatide alternatives have an edge. We need a clear GLP-1 strategy.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Define our GLP-1 strategy now** — compounded window is closed. Do we partner with a brand-name manufacturer (like Ro did with Novo Nordisk)? Do we focus on tirzepatide alternatives? This is urgent.

2. **Lean into the "full stack" narrative** — no one else does TRT + peptides + GLP-1 + AI platform + in-person. Make this the core positioning.

3. **Investigate the "Valhalla Health" name conflict** — the clinical automation company in the MaineHealth cohort is using our brand name. Worth a legal look.

4. **Monitor Defy Medical patient complaints** — communication delays and shipping issues are their Achilles heel. That's where we poach.

5. **Price anchoring** — position above Henry Meds ($129) but below Defy ($250+). Target $149–$199/month all-inclusive with labs as the sweet spot.

6. **Pharmacy partner audit** — with Empower and Hallandale pulling back on GLP-1s, assess what Thrive Rx and DiRX can still legally offer and where the gaps are.
